# Senior Product Designer Technical Assessment Questions
**Version 4.0** — 2026-06-11. Q1-30 unchanged from v3.0 (answers preserved). Q31-80 restructured against the Staff/Principal $250k bar: cut tool trivia, expanded AI-native product design, added accessibility, experimentation, and executive communication.

## SECTION 1: PRODUCT THINKING
* **Question 1:** Your product's conversion rate drops by 15% over the last quarter. Walk me through your first 48 hours.
* **Question 2:** How do you distinguish a UX problem from a business problem?
* **Question 3:** Tell me about a product decision that improved a business outcome. How did you measure success?
* **Question 4:** What is the difference between an output and an outcome? Give a real example.
* **Question 5:** What makes a product successful?

## SECTION 2: PRODUCT STRATEGY
* **Question 6:** How do you define an MVP?
* **Question 7:** What is NOT an MVP?
* **Question 8:** Your CEO asks for ten new features. How do you decide what gets built?
* **Question 9:** How do you prioritize competing stakeholder requests?
* **Question 10:** What prioritization frameworks have you used? How?

## SECTION 3: DISCOVERY
* **Question 11:** What is Product Discovery?
* **Question 12:** Describe your discovery process from start to finish.
* **Question 13:** A stakeholder requests a new feature. What happens next?
* **Question 14:** How do you validate assumptions?
* **Question 15:** How do you know when you have enough evidence to move forward?

## SECTION 4: USER RESEARCH
* **Question 16:** When should you run user interviews?
* **Question 17:** When should you NOT run user interviews?
* **Question 18:** Tell me about a research project you conducted.
* **Question 19:** How many users are enough for usability testing? Why?
* **Question 20:** What are the biggest mistakes researchers make?

## SECTION 5: UX METRICS
* **Question 21:** What UX metrics do you track most often?
* **Question 22:** Which is more important: Task Success Rate or Time on Task? Why?
* **Question 23:** You have Version A (90% Success Rate, 120 sec Time on Task, 4.8/5 CSAT) and Version B (95% Success Rate, 90 sec Time on Task, 3.1/5 CSAT). Which would you choose?
* **Question 24:** Explain SUS.
* **Question 25:** Explain the HEART framework.

## SECTION 6: DESIGN SYSTEMS
* **Question 26:** What is a Design System?
* **Question 27:** How is a Design System different from a UI Kit?
* **Question 28:** How do you govern Design Systems?
* **Question 29:** A VP wants a custom component outside the system. What do you do?
* **Question 30:** Tell me about a Design System challenge you solved.

## SECTION 7: ENGINEERING
* **Question 31:** What makes a handoff successful?
* **Question 32:** How do you make accessibility a non-negotiable part of your process without slowing the team down?
* **Question 33:** What information should exist in a user flow?
* **Question 34:** How do you document edge cases?
* **Question 35:** Engineering estimates 4 months. Product wants 4 weeks. What do you do?

## SECTION 8: ANALYTICS
* **Question 36:** What is the difference between a metric and a KPI?
* **Question 37:** What is a North Star Metric?
* **Question 38:** How would you measure activation?
* **Question 39:** How would you measure retention?
* **Question 40:** Design an A/B test for a checkout redesign. What is your hypothesis, your success metric, and your guardrail metrics?

## SECTION 9: LEADERSHIP
* **Question 41:** Tell me about a conflict with engineering.
* **Question 42:** Tell me about a conflict with a stakeholder.
* **Question 43:** How do you align teams that disagree?
* **Question 44:** How do you influence without authority?
* **Question 45:** You must present a controversial design decision to executives. How do you structure that conversation?

## SECTION 10: AI-NATIVE PRODUCT DESIGN
* **Question 46:** How has AI changed your design process?
* **Question 47:** Which parts of Product Design should be automated — and which should never be?
* **Question 48:** You are designing a feature powered by an LLM that is sometimes wrong. How does that change your UX decisions?
* **Question 49:** What makes users trust — or abandon — an AI feature after the first week?
* **Question 50:** How do you know your AI feature is actually good — before and after shipping? Explain evals like a designer.
* **Question 51:** You are designing an agent that can act on the user's behalf. How do you decide when it asks permission versus acting autonomously?
* **Question 52:** When is chat the wrong interface for an AI product?
* **Question 53:** How do model choice, latency, and token cost change the UX of an AI feature?

## SECTION 11: CLAUDE CODE & AI TOOLING
* **Question 54:** How would you use Claude Code in a real product design workflow without outsourcing your judgment?
* **Question 55:** Describe a design-to-code workflow where AI helps you move from Figma or a product idea to a working prototype.
* **Question 56:** How do you review AI-generated code or UI before trusting it?
* **Question 57:** What risks appear when designers rely too heavily on AI coding tools?

## SECTION 12: FIGMA MCP & AI AGENTS
* **Question 58:** What is MCP, and why does it matter for designers working with AI agents?
* **Question 59:** How would a Figma MCP workflow improve design-system maintenance or design-to-code handoff?
* **Question 60:** If an AI agent can inspect Figma files, what instructions and constraints would you give it?
* **Question 61:** How would you explain AI agents to a product team that has never used them?

## SECTION 13: FRONTEND FOUNDATIONS
* **Question 62:** Why should a product designer understand React?
* **Question 63:** What TypeScript concepts matter most for a designer collaborating with engineers?
* **Question 64:** How do you decide between shadcn/ui, raw Radix primitives, or a fully custom component?
* **Question 65:** What does Tailwind change about designer-developer collaboration?

## SECTION 14: DESIGN SYSTEMS IN CODE
* **Question 66:** How would you explain design tokens to a PM, a designer, and a frontend engineer?
* **Question 67:** What should exist in Storybook for a mature design system?
* **Question 68:** How do you keep Figma components, tokens, and production components aligned over time?
* **Question 69:** What are the signs that a design system is creating friction instead of leverage?
* **Question 70:** How would you measure design-system adoption and impact?

## SECTION 15: PRODUCT ANALYTICS & SHIPPING
* **Question 71:** What can PostHog tell you that a usability test cannot?
* **Question 72:** How would you define an event taxonomy for a new onboarding flow?
* **Question 73:** When should a designer care about feature flags, experiments, and session replay?
* **Question 74:** How would you use Supabase and Vercel to prototype or ship a product idea?

## SECTION 16: PROTOTYPING, MOTION & DATA
* **Question 75:** When would you use Framer, ProtoPie, Rive, or a coded prototype?
* **Question 76:** How would you use Recharts or D3 in a product-design context?
* **Question 77:** An AI tool generates a full UI in seconds. Walk me through how you take it to production quality — and what your role is in that world.

## SECTION 17: INTERVIEW & MARKET POSITIONING
* **Question 78:** Walk me through one portfolio case end-to-end, as in a real interview: problem, constraints, key decisions, outcomes — and what failed or what you would do differently.
* **Question 79:** Why is the combination of UX Designer, Design Systems, React, AI Agents, MCP, Claude Code, and Product Strategy commercially valuable?
* **Question 80:** Pitch yourself: why should we hire you over another Senior Product Designer for a role that wants strategy, design systems, code prototyping, and AI-native workflows?

- Old Q45 (describe your leadership style) — generic; replaced by executive communication.

- Old Q52 (Cursor vs Claude Code) — vendor trivia; judgment covered by Q54/Q56.

- Old Q49 (disagree and commit) — overlapped with Q41/Q42 conflict stories; replaced by Q78 case walkthrough.

**Added (gaps vs Staff/Principal market bar, 2026):**
- Q32 accessibility as process (zero a11y coverage before).
- Q40 experiment design + guardrail metrics (experimentation literacy).
- Q45 presenting controversial decisions to executives (exec communication).
- Q48-Q53 AI-native product design: non-determinism UX, trust curves, evals, agent autonomy/permissions, chat-as-wrong-interface, cost/latency as design constraint. AI fluency carries a +56% wage premium; the old set had only 3 generic AI questions.
- Q77 AI-generated UI to production quality (the designer's post-generation role).
- Q78 end-to-end case walkthrough with failure retro (the most common senior interview moment).
