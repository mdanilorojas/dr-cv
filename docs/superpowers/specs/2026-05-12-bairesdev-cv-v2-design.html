01 · context

Problem

BairesDev triggered a staffing-questionnaire flow on 2026-05-12. Two artifacts drive how they sell Danilo to US enterprise clients — and right now they contradict each other.

The tension

The portal reads as a conservative mid-senior frontend + UX/UI generalist (React 1+, JavaScript 1+, no AI skills declared). The CV reads as a founder-operator *Agentic Designer · Product Engineer*. A recruiter comparing the two detects dissonance — and the matching engine will never route Danilo to AI-forward positions.

03 · decisions

Architectural decisions

1 · Positioning — agentic forward, frontend as reinforcement

Before (current thesis)

"I ship real products — and I ship the tools agents use to help me ship them."

After (bairesdev variant only)

"Agentic Designer. 15 years delivering product — today I ship SaaS, design systems, and frontend with agents as force multiplier."

Reframes Danilo for a recruiter who parses the CV linearly: agentic identity → 15 years proof → delivery today. Keeps the agentic pitch in the first 20 words of the summary.

2 · Sidebar — agentic group first, inventory on page 2

The sidebar reorders to Agents & AI-Assisted Delivery → Design & Systems → Engineering → Strategy. Page 2 gains a new Skills Inventory block that mirrors the BairesDev taxonomy (skill + years), positioned in the left column below Education.

3 · Baseline — "realista amplio"

For AI skills, count honest years of practice (including pre-Claude-Code LLM work). For classical skills, count from first documented professional use (Tecniequipos 2013 for JS/HTML/PHP, Xentinels 2016 for Figma/DS/Agile).
