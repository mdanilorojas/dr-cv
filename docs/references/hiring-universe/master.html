§ 01Executive summary

The arc of 10 rounds, the final pick, why.

V11 began with a market-level question — what does the tier above Booz Allen Hamilton actually filter on in 2026? — and ended with a direction that refuses a hero image, refuses a CTA button, and leads with 2,000 words. R1 surveyed 17 target employers (Anthropic, OpenAI, Stripe, Figma, Linear, Cursor, Vercel, Perplexity, Replit, Cohere, Mistral, Airbnb, Runway, Notion, IDEO, Pentagram, Work & Co) and extracted twelve shared filters, five of which Danilo's landing must emit — craft + taste claim, writing as first-class artifact, agent-native proof, live interactive element, and profile-plus-agents framing. R2 read 14 portfolios of people already inside those rooms and found that 9 of 14 run the Essayist shape, not the case grid — the Chiang template is the median, not the differentiator.

R3 catalogued the 2026 agentic-UX vocabulary (artifact · thinking · skill · plan · diff · project) so the landing would demonstrate fluency rather than buzzword-stuff. R4 dissected 12 case studies and named Narrative Ship as the right shape — Context → Problem → Approach → Ship → Outcome → What-I'd-do-differently → Craft Floor. R5 tested three voice registers on Danilo's actual positioning and landed on Draft C (taste-first critic): "Most teams are still designing for AI. I design with it." R6 audited Danilo's proof surface against the median-hire baseline from R2 and found two axes where he can eclipse it — agentic-specific (column B) and writing (column C, currently empty).

R7 ran craft-floor audits on the existing landing-huly output and found 12 must-fixes (Google Fonts CDN, no skip link, invalid role="marquee", no OG image, no LICENSE, no CI). R8 catalogued ~80 anti-patterns across 8 categories and distilled the top 10 — led by "Do not ship the Huly-clone warm-radial-glow pill CTA" and "Do not claim 'agentic' without a running agent artifact." R9 shortlisted three directions with complete specs and recommended convergence on Direction 1 ("The Notebook") with Direction 3's proof strip grafted below the fold. R10 is the full build spec: copy verbatim in EN + ES, palette + typography + motion spec, 8-task production plan merge-safe from the existing huly-landing template, explicit refusals of all 10 R8 anti-patterns, and a 4-week rollout tying R6's five high-leverage proofs to the ship date 2026-06-05.

The bet is simple and contrarian: the Chiang template is 2026's baseline, the Huly-clone is 2026's tell, and long-form writing is the single under-used axis where a Latin American designer can visibly clear the bar without competing on demo-reel production value. The essay IS the landing. The cases become footnotes. Proof strip sits quietly below. Testimonials embed where they earn their weight. Every craft floor from R7 cleared; every anti-pattern from R8 explicitly refused.

§ 03Findings timeline · how conclusions evolved

The research didn't land on The Notebook in round 1. It triangulated across 9 rounds before R10 wrote the build spec.

R01 · signal extraction

The market is filtering on craft, writing, and agent-native — not on AI buzzwords.

17 target employers surveyed. Every one filters on the word craft. Anthropic explicitly asks for blog posts at the top of the résumé. Stripe is hiring "Senior Staff Product Designer, Agentic Commerce" by name. 5 must-emit signals distilled.

R02 · shape discovery

The median portfolio at tier-1 is the Essayist, not the Curated Grid.

14 portfolios of hired ICs. 9 Essayist-Builder (Paco, Rauno, Pedro, Shu, Rasmus, Lee Robinson). Only 2 Curated Grid (Chiang, Kraft). Writing outperforms case studies at senior tier — which changes the whole landing shape.

R03 · vocabulary audit

2026 has a specific agentic-UX vocabulary. Fluency is table-stakes.

10 patterns across 14 shipped products. Artifact, Thinking, Skill, Plan, Diff-review, Checkpoint. The landing demonstrates command of this vocabulary in every case study and in the essay — named vocabulary beats buzzword soup.

R04 · case-study format

Narrative Ship is the senior-vs-mid discriminator.

Of 12 case studies reviewed, the ones that read senior end with "What I'd Do Differently" and a craft floor strip. Bootcamp redesigns and Dribbble concept shots are disqualifying.

R05 · voice register

Danilo should sound like a taste-first critic, not an evangelist.

Three register drafts tested. Draft B (Evangelist) puts him in a crowded room with every Vercel-adjacent dev. Draft C (Taste-first critic) is the voice that sells a POV — and Danilo's differentiator is a POV.

R06 · proof audit

Writing is Danilo's most under-used axis. The essay is the lever.

12×6 matrix against median-hire baseline. Two columns where Danilo can eclipse: agentic-specific (col B) and writing (col C, currently empty). 5 highest-leverage proofs — domain, LinkedIn recs, essay, public skill, Loom.

R07 · craft floor

The landing-huly output has 12 must-fixes before it can ship as V11.

Self-host fonts (#1), remove role="marquee", add skip link, OG image, LICENSE, CI, absolute hreflang. The landing IS the work sample — every leak is evaluated as taste failure.

R08 · anti-patterns

The current landing silhouette IS the Huly-clone. That has to change.

~80 anti-patterns catalogued. Top 10 distilled. #1 is "Don't ship the warm-radial-glow pill CTA." The existing dr-cv landing is that exact silhouette — recruiters recognize it in 0.5s as template.

R09 · shortlist

Three internally-coherent bets. Recommendation: D1 with D3 proof strip.

D1 Notebook (essay-anchored, highest ceiling, 10–14 days) · D2 Workshop (live demo, 12–18 days, infra-heavy) · D3 Shipping Log (Chiang template, safest baseline but same as everyone). Convergence: D1 + D3's proof strip grafted below the fold.

Essay in EN + ES parallel-drafted. 4 cases in Narrative Ship format. Proof strip grafted. Two testimonials placed where they earn their weight. Evolves generators/templates/huly-landing/ in 8 merge-safe tasks. Ship window: 10–14 days.

A working notebook on agentic product design — four shipped cases, fifteen years of making software before any of this was called agentic, and a thesis that is still moving because the medium still is.

Essay · 14 min read 4 cases · EN / ES Last revised 2026-05-08

There is a sentence I keep deleting and rewriting, and it is this: the phrase "AI designer" is a category error. It implies a designer whose object of study is artificial intelligence — the way a typography designer's object is letterforms. That is not what most of us are actually doing. What most of us are doing, whether we name it or not, is designing the relationship between a human maker, the model they are working with, and the artifact the two co-author together.

I have spent the last year testing that reframe in four projects that all shipped. One runs paying pilots for a compliance startup in Quito. One is a design system that a Claude sub-agent can read, invoke, and render — it is, in the literal sense, an agent-consumable skill. One is a dark-first DS whose accessibility rules live inside the tokens themselves. And one is a consumer app with Gemini as the runtime primitive — not a chatbot bolted on.

§ 05Decision defense

The Chiang-template (Direction 3) is 2026's median — a safe yield but not a differentiator, because every competent senior already ships it. The Workshop (Direction 2) is the most exciting demo but 14 days of infrastructure work that has nothing to do with design judgment and drags a risk of dead-demo on the day a recruiter opens the tab. The Notebook (Direction 1) bets on the one axis Danilo can own unreplicated — a first-person thesis on agentic design that no copycat can reproduce and no agent can fake — while D3's proof-strip graft closes the "all talk, where's the work" risk by putting 5 click-to-verify numbers one scroll below the essay. R5 explicit: "Pick Draft C, borrow Draft A's restraint for the rest of the landing." R9 explicit: "Converge on Direction 1 with Direction 3's proof strip." R10 is that convergence, written to ship.
