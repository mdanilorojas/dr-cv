The Notebook / thesis no. 01, still moving

Most teams are still designing for AI.
I design with it.

A working notebook on agentic product design — four shipped cases, fifteen years of making software before any of this was called agentic, and a thesis that is still moving on purpose.

Essay · 14 min read 4 cases · EN / ES Last revised 2026-05-08

There is a sentence I keep deleting and rewriting, and it is this: the word "AI designer" is a category error. It implies a designer whose object of study is artificial intelligence — the way a typography designer's object is letterforms. That is not what most of us are actually doing. What most of us are doing, whether we name it or not, is designing the relationship between a human maker, the model they are working with, and the artifact the two co-author together.

I have spent the last year testing that reframe in four projects that all shipped. One of them runs paying pilots for a compliance startup in Quito. One of them is a design system that a Claude sub-agent can read, invoke, and render — it is, in the literal sense, an agent-consumable skill. One is a dark-first DS whose accessibility rules live in the tokens themselves, so any agent that touches the tokens cannot emit an inaccessible interface. And one is a consumer app with Gemini as the runtime…

03The decision question

If you can answer this single question honestly, R10 writes itself.

The strategic question for R10

Is Danilo's strongest hireable asset his POV, his demo, or his track record?

The three directions map 1:1 to these three answers. The question is not which asset is strongest in absolute terms — all three are real. It is which asset, when placed first, best differentiates Danilo from the median tier-1 candidate in May 2026. The median candidate has a track record. Fewer have a demo. Very few have a published POV.

If the answer is POV
