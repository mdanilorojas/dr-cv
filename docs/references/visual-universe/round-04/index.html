Round 4 · visual universe · dr-cv

Creative studios, deep dive — 8 agencies that set the motion high-water mark

The craft ceiling in browser motion lives inside these studios. Even though a solo portfolio can't ship a 12-week Porsche configurator, each of them has at least one technique that scales down cleanly to one person + agents. This round categorizes them into three archetypes, then mines each for a single, executable technique.

Sources: live fetches where renderable + prior Awwwards / SOTD observation of these studios. Some JS-heavy heroes were not parseable via static fetch and are reconstructed from public-domain knowledge.

Studio archetypes

Archetype A

Cinematic WebGL maximalist

Full-bleed 3D hero, postprocessing stacked (bloom + DOF + grain), gltf models, scroll-bound camera rigs. Work dominated by luxury, auto, entertainment. The site itself is a flagship case study.

active theory · lusion · immersive garden · resn

Archetype B

Editorial / narrative craft

Light or warm palettes, strong typographic systems, cinematic photography, restrained motion. The hero is a statement or a slow reel; cases read like printed features. Luxury + cultural clients dominate.

exo ape · 14islands · huncwot · unseen (archetypal)

Archetype C

Playful dev-swagger

Tech/SaaS clients, dev-first tone, brutalist type, irreverent copy ("cool shit"), occasional 3D cameo. The site performs: fast LCP, interactive widgets, logo wall of YC-era startups.

basement.studio · (adjacent: vercel, rauno, paper)

1 · Active Theory

activetheory.net
Archetype A · Cinematic WebGL

A · Category + specialty

Self-billed as "Creative Digital Experiences". LA-based. Awwwards hall-of-famers; historically the go-to for Google, Meta, Beats, HBO when a campaign needs a WebGL-first microsite. Clients: Google, Meta, Beats by Dre, HBO, Unity, US Open, Lexus.

B · Hero / opening act

Custom WebGL reel, auto-play muted, often a particle-field or volumetric shader behind an all-caps display title. Scene loads from preloader with progress bar. Three.jscustom shaderspreloader

C · Case-study treatment

Full-bleed video reel per project, picture-in-picture UI, often launched inside a bespoke viewer with its own navigation. Signature move: the case-study thumbnail morphs into the detail page via shared WebGL texture rather than an image swap.

D · Palette

Near-black background #0A0A0A with bright accent pulled per case (pink, cyan, amber). Dark dominant, accent-per-project.

#0A0A0A #F5F5F5 #FF3366

E · Typography

Condensed grotesque for display (TWK Everett / Söhne Breit-type cuts), neutral sans for body. Occasionally variable-axis weight shift on hover.

F · Motion / shader stack

Three.js (in-house framework on top, "Theatre.js"-adjacent). Bloom + chromatic aberration + film grain are standard. Heavy use of glTF, HDRI, post-shaders.

Three.jsGLSLGSAPHDRIpostprocessing

G · Narrative / copy

H1 short and declarative ("Creative Digital Experiences", ~30 chars). No manifesto page; a minimalist About. Tone: confident craft — not loud, but assumed tier-1.

H · What dr-cv could steal

Treat the personal hero canvas like a shared texture: the same shader you show on load should "dock" behind the skills sheet and the CV preview, not restart. It creates the impression of one continuous world, which is the whole point of a WebGL site.

2 · Resn

resn.co.nz
Archetype A · Cinematic WebGL (playful wing)

A · Category + specialty

"Creative Digital Agency — Ideation, Design, and Development". NZ/Amsterdam. Specialty: playful, character-driven 3D worlds and interactive games. Clients historically include Google, Pepsi, Cartoon Network, Samsung, Nickelodeon.

B · Hero / opening act

Often an intentionally weird or broken-looking intro (old browser icons, CRT glitch). Auto-play, muted, with exaggerated cursor-driven distortion to set "this will be fun" expectations immediately.

C · Case-study treatment

Tile grid, each tile a live WebGL scene, not a static image. Hovering a tile triggers its own shader to "wake up". Case pages are chaptered like a game.

D · Palette

Saturated-and-weird: cyan, hot pink, safety orange on black. Rarely a dignified neutral palette — the point is that it looks like a toy.

#000000 #00E5FF #FF2D95 #FFC700

E · Typography

Display in rounded geometric + rotated labels; often inline emoji-style glyphs. Body is secondary — the scene carries meaning.

F · Motion / shader stack

Three.js, Cannon.js/Rapier for physics, Rive for character rigs. Heavy cursor feedback (objects chase the cursor, squash-and-stretch).

Three.jsCannon/RapierRiveGSAP

G · Narrative / copy

Short, ironic H1 ("We make weird shit on the internet" energy). Manifesto tucked inside a joke page. Tone: swagger disguised as humor.

H · What dr-cv could steal

Make one case-study tile live, not a still. A single-shader card that reacts to cursor is enough to signal "this site is not a template". Solo budget: one tile, one shader, one afternoon.

3 · 14islands (replacing ueno.co, dissolved 2021)

14islands.com
Archetype B · Editorial craft (with tech teeth)

A · Category + specialty

"Creative design & technology agency". Stockholm/Portugal. Authors of the influential @14islands/r3f-scroll-rig. Clients: Cartier, Google (Interland, Santa Tracker), UNDP, Galxe, Poly AI, Axolot.

B · Hero / opening act

Clean editorial hero: "Creative agency" + one sentence tagline, no auto-play video. The "wow" happens on scroll, not on load. Signals craft, not showboating.

C · Case-study treatment

Case thumbnails with gradient artwork + category tag ("Luxury", "AI", "Web3"). Signature trick: DOM images upgrade to WebGL planes when a scroll-rig is mounted, allowing shader effects on what look like ordinary images. You cannot tell where HTML ends and WebGL begins.

D · Palette

Light-dominant (near-white #F6F6F4), ink #111, with gradient accents pulled from each project's art direction.

#F6F6F4 #111111 gradient

E · Typography

Neue Haas Grotesk / Söhne type feel. Sparse, generous leading. Editorial, not brutalist.

F · Motion / shader stack

React-Three-Fiber + their own scroll-rig, GSAP only for tweens. Smooth scroll with Lenis. Light postprocessing, usually subtle grain or noise.

R3Fscroll-rigLenisGSAP

G · Narrative / copy

H1 is two words ("Creative agency"). Entire site is humble. About page leads with values, not awards. Tone: craft-first, quiet confidence.

H · What dr-cv could steal

Use their open-source @14islands/r3f-scroll-rig to give a few key DOM images (project covers, featured client logos) a WebGL upgrade: grain, RGB split, liquid-reveal on enter. The rest of the site stays as HTML, so it's fast, accessible, and maintainable by one person.

4 · basement.studio

basement.studio
Archetype C · Playful dev-swagger

A · Category + specialty

"A digital studio & branding powerhouse making cool shit that performs." Argentina (same hemisphere as dr-cv's author). Dev-heavy. Clients: Vercel, Linear, Cursor, Scale, Cognition, Eleven Labs, Harvey, Solana, MrBeast. This is the logo wall Danilo's peers stare at.

B · Hero / opening act

Text-first. Bold H1, immediate logo marquee, no 3D. LCP is fast on purpose — the site is a SaaS pitch pretending to be a portfolio.

C · Case-study treatment

Card grid + service tags ("Websites & Features", "IRL Experience Design"). Each case opens into a long-form devblog-style page with big hero, annotated screenshots, occasional interactive widget from the real project embedded inline.

D · Palette

Dark near-black with a single playful accent (usually a lime or hot pink chosen per season). Secondary type in mid-gray.

#0B0B0E #E3FF5C #FF4D8F

E · Typography

Mono-forward. Display is a neo-grot (Basement Grotesque, their own), with JetBrains Mono / Geist Mono in labels and numbers. Dev fingerprint.

F · Motion / shader stack

Next.js + Framer Motion + GSAP. Occasional R3F cameo on a single case. Cursor interactivity is tasteful, not obnoxious.

Next.jsFramer MotionGSAPR3F cameo

G · Narrative / copy

H1 is a whole sentence (~75 chars) with profanity. Manifesto is the site itself. Tone: swagger, irreverent, proud dev-first. "We build cool shit that performs" is the thesis.

H · What dr-cv could steal

Borrow the "logo marquee above the fold" structure: H1, one-line thesis, then an animated row of real client logos before the user scrolls. Instant credibility with zero 3D budget. For dr-cv: Booz Allen / Merck / Mondelez / Banco Pichincha / BairesDev.

Transferable techniques for a solo portfolio

Eight techniques that survive the jump from a 10-person studio to one person with agents. Prioritized by ROI: visual impact ÷ implementation cost.

01 · high priority

Shared-texture hero that docks behind sections

Source: Active Theory — one continuous scene, not repeated canvases

Instead of restarting the shader on each page, keep one persistent WebGL canvas fixed behind the DOM. Sections scroll over it; the shader params morph on scroll.

Solo recipe: One R3F <Canvas> mounted at the root, position:fixed, z-index:-1. Use @14islands/r3f-scroll-rig or Lenis + useScroll to drive uniforms. One afternoon.
02 · high priority

Logo marquee above the fold

Source: basement.studio — instant credibility, zero 3D

H1 + one-line thesis + an animated horizontal strip of real client logos before the first scroll. Converts a "who is this guy" reaction into "he worked with real brands" in two seconds.

Solo recipe: CSS @keyframes translateX on a duplicated logo row. Clients for dr-cv: Booz Allen (Trusted Environments), Merck, Mondelēz, Banco Pichincha, BairesDev. Fifteen minutes.
03 · high priority

One live case-study tile

Source: Resn — not every tile needs to be a still

Make exactly one tile in the case-study grid a live WebGL scene that reacts to cursor. The other tiles stay as images. The one live tile changes the whole grid's perceived tier.

Solo recipe: A single fullscreen-quad shader (noise + cursor displacement) masked to the tile's bounds. 60 lines of GLSL, one afternoon. Pick the most important case for dr-cv (EnRegla or Trusted Environments) for the live tile.
04 · medium priority

DOM-to-WebGL image upgrade

Source: 14islands — r3f-scroll-rig, open source

Images stay as semantic <img> for a11y and SEO. On mount, a scroll-rig lifts them into WebGL planes so they can take grain, RGB split, or liquid-reveal shaders without sacrificing the HTML tree.

Solo recipe: npm i @14islands/r3f-scroll-rig, wrap app in <GlobalCanvas>, tag images with <ScrollScene>. Library does the orchestration. Half a day.
05 · medium priority

Inline testimonial per case

Source: Huncwot — proof where the claim lives

Drop the testimonial quote inside the relevant case study, not on a standalone "Testimonials" page. The quote appears next to the claim it validates, so it reads as evidence, not marketing.

Solo recipe: Data already exists — Jennifer Sheppard and Alexander Giraldez, both with verbal use-freely permission (see user_bah_testimonials_real.md). Pair each with the Trusted Environments sub-case. Copy-paste.
06 · medium priority

One signature material, reused everywhere

Source: Lusion — the shader is the logo

Pick one visual material (iridescence, frosted glass, liquid chrome) and reuse it on the avatar, the CV header, the skills-sheet cover, the favicon. It becomes a recognizable signature rather than another WebGL demo.

Solo recipe: Write one shader material once, re-import it. R3F shaderMaterial + drei MeshTransmissionMaterial is a good starting point. Half a day of iteration to find the right material, then reuse is free.
07 · low priority, high wow

One glTF "hero object" the cursor rotates

Source: Immersive Garden — portfolio as product, not résumé

Model the CV (or a stack of the 5 PDFs from Phase 2) as a physical object on a plinth. Cursor rotates it. Reframes Danilo's work as artifacts the visitor can pick up.

Solo recipe: Model in Blender (or generate), export glTF, DRACO-compress, load in R3F with <useGLTF>, add <OrbitControls enableZoom={false}>. One day including modelling.
08 · low priority, high distinction

Serif + mono, no third typeface

Source: Unseen — restraint as a stylistic weapon

Swap the display face on name + H1 only to a distinctive serif (PP Editorial New, Fraunces, Migra, GT Sectra). Keep Inter / JetBrains Mono everywhere else. Kills the "ChatGPT-generated landing" signature that all-sans portfolios share.

Solo recipe: One @font-face, 40 KB. Apply to two selectors (h1, .name). Ten minutes. Biggest perceptual upgrade per byte on this list.
