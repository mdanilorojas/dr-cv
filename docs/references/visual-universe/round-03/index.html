Designer/engineer portfolios · deep dive

Ten personal portfolios from working designers and engineers, studied with the specific goal of informing the visual language of dr-cv (Danilo Rojas' "Agentic Designer" landing). These are the most directly relevant references in the 10-round universe because our own surface IS a portfolio.

Date 2026-05-08 Reference baseline huly.io (scraped in round 01) Stack notes detected where markup exposed it Accent #FF8964

01Three archetypes

After reading all 10 sites back-to-back, the space splits cleanly into three stances. Each makes a different trade between spectacle, substance, and voice. dr-cv can pick one and commit, or (more interesting) pick two and let them collide.

Archetype A

Maximalist WebGL playground

The portfolio itself is the case study. 3D scene, physics, a "hero moment" that makes you DM the link. Risk: looks like a technical flex, not a hire-me pitch.

bruno-simon.com robin-noguier.com
Archetype B

Developer-terminal minimalist

Content-first, one viewport wide, opinionated type, near-zero ornament. The craft is in the micro-interactions, not the surface. This is the Vercel / Linear / Raycast house style.

brittanychiang.com paco.me rauno.me ped.ro emilkowal.ski jhey.dev
Archetype C

Editorial / literary garden

Long scroll, first-person voice, writing treated as equal to work. Lowercase headers, decorative glyphs, a "dear friend" tone. Portfolio as zine, not product page.

olivierlarose.com henry.codes

03Five recommendations for dr-cv

Each recommendation is tied to a specific source site + a specific technique we'd adapt. None of these is "copy the whole thing" — they're discrete moves we can compose.

01

Pick archetype B with one "hero moment" from archetype A

SOURCE · brittanychiang.com + robin-noguier.com

Ship a developer-terminal baseline (Brittany's fixed-left-bio + scrolling-right-sections pattern) so the site is readable, accessible, and signals seriousness. Then invest in exactly ONE WebGL/Canvas moment in the hero — not a game, just a single motion gesture that reacts to presence (agent-avatar breathing, a tokenized gradient reshaping on scroll).

WHY: serio-cálido brand can't afford maximalist chaos; but a single lit candle in a quiet room signals "I can do this when it matters."
02

Inline interactive demos inside case studies — not screenshots

SOURCE · emilkowal.ski

For each case study (EnRegla, Aura, Life Update, Trusted Environments DS), embed a working widget that illustrates the agent intervention: a token diff, a before/after slider, a live command-palette where the visitor asks the agent to "generate a variant."

WHY: our thesis ("profile + agentic AI = combination") is invisible in static screenshots. A working 10-line demo PROVES the method in a way copy cannot.
03

Ship a "Craft" subpage of interaction micro-experiments

SOURCE · rauno.me/craft

A dedicated URL (dr-cv.dev/craft) with 6-12 small interactive pieces: agent-driven form validation, a live prompt-to-component demo, a tokenized theming playground, a diff viewer. Each piece links to the underlying repo.

WHY: decouples "proof of craft" from "case studies we can't fully share" (BAH NDA). Craft lives publicly; case studies can stay summary-only.
04

Set client names as typographic furniture, not logo wall

SOURCE · henry.codes

Instead of a "Trusted by" logo grid (corporate, generic), render the client list as large quiet display type: BOOZ ALLEN · MERCK · MONDELĒZ · BANCO PICHINCHA. Fade each on scroll-in. Feels editorial, bypasses the BAH-logo-permission problem, and reads as confident.

WHY: we already flagged the BAH logo can't be published. Typography sidesteps it AND looks better than the alternative.
05

Add a "Now" page and live personal signals

SOURCE · paco.me + jhey.dev

A /now page describing what Danilo is currently focused on (quarterly updates). Plus a small real-time strip in the footer: current project, commit of the day, "last deploy: 2 hours ago." Agent-generated, refreshed automatically from the github repo.

WHY: the /now page is the highest-trust conversion element among the 10 sites we read. Live repo signals also dogfood the "agentic" thesis — the site updates itself.
