02Typography · five type system archetypes rendered at scale

Each specimen below renders Danilo's real thesis — "I ship real products. Agents give me the leverage." — at full hero scale, in the type system of that archetype. Differences are visible to the eye, not described. Every font here has a free web-available alternative; proprietary faces (esbuild, Söhne, Styrene) have named substitutes.

T1 · Mono display + Inter body (the Huly pattern)

JetBrains Mono 700 · Inter 400 · JetBrains Mono 500 (labels)

Agentic Designer
· Product Engineer.

I ship real products — and I ship the tools agents use to help me ship them.

Used by

huly.io, basement.studio (Basement Grotesque + mono labels), vercel (Geist Mono), replicate (code demos).

Why it works

Mono in the display position signals "this is written by an engineer" instantly. Zero claim, visual proof.

H1 spec

64px / 700 / -0.04em / 0.95 line-height. Body 16–18px Inter 400, 1.55 leading.

Free stack

JetBrains Mono (Google), Geist Mono (Vercel OSS), or Commit Mono. Avoid "esbuild" (Huly proprietary).

Risk

Can read "devtool", not "designer" if over-applied. Keep body in Inter so prose stays readable.

dr-cv rhythm

H1 mono. H2 mono. Body Inter. Labels mono 11–12px uppercase. Testimonials Inter italic for contrast.

T2 · Variable sans · extreme-axis play

Inter Variable · weight 800 · width condensed · -0.045em tracking

Agentic Designer.
Product Engineer.

I ship real products — and I ship the tools agents use to help me ship them.

Used by

linear.app (Inter Variable -0.02em), vercel (Geist very tight), cursor (system 700–800).

Why it works

One typeface, many voices. The variation itself becomes the system — no family collision.

H1 spec

72px / 800 / -0.045em / 0.98. Subhead same family 300 weight. That weight contrast IS the rhythm.

Free stack

Inter Variable, Geist, Space Grotesk. Inter is free and identical to Linear's stack.

Risk

Too generic. Half the web ships this. Distinction must come from palette or layout, not type.

dr-cv rhythm

If used: require one distinctive move (tightness -0.045, or 800 weight, or a single condensed axis) to avoid "looks AI-generated."

T3 · Serif editorial + grotesk body

Fraunces / Instrument Serif display · Inter 400 body · mono metadata

Agentic Designer
· Product Engineer.

I ship real products — and I ship the tools agents use to help me ship them.

Used by

anthropic.com (Styrene + Tiempos), huncwot.com (GT Sectra vibes), unseen.studio (ABC Arizona / PP Editorial), henry.codes.

Why it works

A serif display signals "I read books, I am not another devtool." Instantly reads editorial, warm, institutional.

H1 spec

72px / 500 / -0.025em / 1.02. Fraunces at opsz 144 is gorgeous. Italic cut for the subtitle = free elegance.

Free stack

Fraunces (Google, variable), Instrument Serif (Google), Lora, or self-host PP Editorial New (licensed).

Risk

Can read "wellness startup" if the accent colour is too pastel. Terracotta + charcoal grounds it.

dr-cv rhythm

Serif on name + H1 only. Inter everywhere else. Don't let serif leak into UI chrome or it stops being a signature.

T4 · All-mono terminal (Chiang / kprverse pattern)

IBM Plex Mono OR JetBrains Mono · single face · colour for state

agentic-designer
· product-engineer

> ship_products() ; ship_tools(agents.ship(products))

Used by

brittanychiang.com (Inter + SF Mono, terminal-adjacent), kprverse.com (true terminal), rauno (mono-leaning).

Why it works

Extreme commitment signals extreme competence. It is the dev-portfolio cliché — but done correctly it reads "I write code for a living."

H1 spec

52px / 500 / -0.025em / 1.1. Blinking caret. Colour reserved for active state only (#64ffda or similar).

Free stack

IBM Plex Mono, JetBrains Mono, Berkeley Mono (licensed), Commit Mono.

Risk

Screams "developer, not designer." For dr-cv this contradicts the "Agentic Designer" positioning. Fatal as the whole site.

dr-cv rhythm

Use only in one section: a live "ship log" terminal strip showing real commits. Not the hero.

T5 · Big serif display · unapologetic editorial

Instrument Serif 400 · 88px display · Inter 300 body · no mono

Agentic Designer
· Product Engineer.

I ship real products — and I ship the tools agents use to help me ship them.

Used by

anthropic.com (closest), henry.codes, and a growing cohort of 2026 YC landing pages reversing the "all sans" trend.

Why it works

At 88px+, Instrument Serif's tall, slightly-condensed forms feel like a Vogue cover crossed with an annual report. High-commitment, high-memorable.

H1 spec

88px / 400 / -0.01em / 1.02. Italic cut on the second line for rhythm. Body at 17px / 300 for the airy contrast.

Free stack

Instrument Serif (Google, free), Fraunces, GT Sectra (licensed).

Risk

Alienates engineer-first buyers. "Is this a designer or a novelist?" — exactly the question we don't want at the hero.

dr-cv rhythm

Reserve for the Bold bet direction only. Requires re-briefing tone as "editorial-serious" rather than "serio + cálido dev."

04Copy voice audit H1 + subhead cross-cut

What verbs do the top agentic landings put in their H1? After surveying the 40+ sites scraped across R1–R4, a clear pattern emerges: "agent(s) + outcome verb" wins. Nouns ("Agentic Designer") feel like a claim; verbs ("Ship agents that…") feel like proof. Linear, LangChain, Inkeep, Cursor all ship the verb pattern. Anthropic and Lusion are the editorial exceptions.

Pattern A · "agent + verb" (dominant)

  • Linear "The product development system for teams and agents"· dark, mono product mock
  • LangChain "Ship agents that work wow"· utility-first, dev-humor
  • Inkeep "Add AI Teammates to your customer experience team"· enterprise positioning
  • Cursor "Agents turn ideas into code" (H2, subhead role)· IDE-forward

Pattern B · editorial claim (rare, high-brand)

  • Anthropic "AI research and products that put safety at the frontier"· cream, serif, editorial
  • Huncwot "Crafting digital products that AI can only dream of"· warm-editorial, typographic
  • Lusion long-form "we create 3D visual storytelling…"· craft-swagger

Pattern C · one-noun claim (minimal)

  • Vercel "Build and deploy on the AI Cloud."· dark, mono
  • Replicate "Run AI with an API."· white, dev-marketing
  • Raycast "Your shortcut to everything."· Apple-adjacent

Pattern D · question (Vercel family)

  • v0.app "What do you want to create?"· prompt-as-hero
  • basement.studio "A digital studio & branding powerhouse making cool shit that performs."· long claim-as-hero
dr-cv audit · current huly-landing H1

"Agentic Designer shipping real products"

Landing copy currently blends Pattern A (the noun "Agentic Designer") with Pattern C (the declarative "shipping real products"). It is on-trend in category (the word "agent" is in the H1 — same move as Linear and LangChain) but it leans on a noun label where peers are leaning on outcome verbs. The thesis in positioning.yaml ("I ship real products — and I ship the tools agents use to help me ship them") is actually the stronger H1 candidate than the current tagline: two verbs, one paradox, zero résumé-speak.

verdict · on-trend, but subhead > H1 on outcome verbs

Three alternative H1s (Pattern A)

01
PATTERN A · agent + verb · closest to Linear

I ship products. Agents give me the leverage.

Uses Danilo's own thesis almost verbatim. Two short verbs bookend the sentence. "Leverage" is the right word: not "replace," not "help," but the thing agents actually do. Recommended.

02
PATTERN A · agent + verb · LangChain-adjacent

Design like an engineer. Ship like ten.

Two imperatives, same rhythm as "Ship agents that work wow." "Ship like ten" translates "agent leverage" into a human-scale benefit without using the word "agent." Higher risk (memorable but potentially too swagger for serio-cálido); test against #1.

03
PATTERN A · "teams and agents" · Linear-adjacent

Design craft for a world of teams and agents.

Direct borrow of Linear's hero pattern but pivoted to design craft. Positions Danilo as the designer for Linear's world. High category alignment — reads as "I speak your language, Linear users." Safer than #2, less distinctive than #1.
