What the market says it wants in 2026

Signal extraction from 17 target employers for an AI Product Designer / Agentic UX Lead role. Source: each company's own careers pages, design-team pages, and published hiring philosophy — fetched 2026-05-08. The goal is a hire at a company tier above BAH.

The common signal language — 2026 edition

Across all 17 pages, the same words appear with suspicious consistency. These are the 12 filters that repeat. If your landing does not visibly answer them in the first scroll, you are invisible to their screeners.

01
Craft / craftsmanship

Linear: "deep care for software craftsmanship." Stripe: "Craft & Beauty." Cursor: "software craftsmanship." Figma: "Love your craft." The single most repeated word.

02
Ship / shipping

Cursor "Ship early." Mistral "ship early and accelerate." Vercel "Ship what's Next." Replit "Momentum wins." Evidence of things delivered beats process talk.

03
Taste

Cursor "know what good looks like." Linear Method: "craft, taste, quality, writing, decisions." Signals aesthetic judgment, not just execution.

04
Writing & research

Anthropic explicitly: "if you've written a thoughtful blog post… put that at the top of your résumé." Linear Method puts writing next to craft as a first-class skill.

05
Open source / public work

Anthropic names it. OpenAI implies it ("independent research, contributed to open source"). Replit's whole brand is public building. A private Dribbble is insufficient.

06
Agents + designers together

Linear: "teams and agents operate together." Stripe has "Senior Staff Product Designer, Agentic Commerce" and "Design Program Manager, AI." Figma: "Product Designer, AI Models." The role category now exists by name.

07
Prototypes in code / Design Engineer

Vercel, Cursor, Stripe ("Design Engineer, Presence"), Linear all carry the title. The role is no longer niche — it is the default for AI-tool startups.

08
0 → 1 / ambiguity tolerance

Cursor "haven for self-motivated individual contributors." Mistral "entrepreneurial spirit." Anthropic "the right person to do it is probably you." They hire operators, not spec-takers.

09
Mission & stakes

Anthropic "safety-first." OpenAI "AGI benefits humanity." Cohere "real-world problems." IDEO "in the AI era, growth depends on people." They filter for why-you-care, not where-you-worked.

10
Rigor / empiricism

Anthropic "do the simple thing that works," "safety is a science." Stripe "be meticulous about the foundations." Distinguishes senior from mid.

11
Low-ego collaboration

Stripe "egoless collaboration," "disagree and commit." Mistral "leave your ego aside." Anthropic "no bystanders." Sole-genius framing is disqualifying.

12
Evidence over credentials

Anthropic verbatim: "we care about what you can do, not where you learned to do it." Replit "Proof matters." The portfolio is the interview.

The 5 signals dr-cv MUST emit

Distilled from 17 careers surfaces. Each one below is a concrete thing Danilo's landing must communicate above the fold, tied to the specific language the target companies filter on.

01
Tied to: Anthropic · Linear · Stripe · Figma · Cursor

Craft + Taste as the top-of-page claim — not the footer

Every single top-tier company filters on the word "craft" (Linear: "deep care for software craftsmanship"; Stripe: "Craft & Beauty"; Cursor: "software craftsmanship"; Figma: "Love your craft"). Taste (Cursor: "know what good looks like") is the senior-differentiator. dr-cv's hero must make a craft+taste statement visible before any project tile — not bury it in an "About" block. Danilo's "Agentic Designer" identity is fine, but the craft claim must be the first adjective the reader meets.
02
Tied to: Anthropic · Replit · Linear Method · IDEO

Writing as a first-class portfolio artifact — not buried metadata

Anthropic verbatim: "if you've written a thoughtful blog post, put that at the top of your resume." Linear Method lists writing next to craft and taste. Replit: "Proof matters." dr-cv must surface at least one piece of long-form writing (essay or case study) at the same visual weight as case-study tiles. This is the single most-underused differentiator for a Latin American designer competing with US ICs who already blog.
03
Tied to: Stripe ("Senior Staff Product Designer, Agentic Commerce") · Figma ("Product Designer, AI Models") · Linear ("teams and agents operate together") · Replit (Agent)

Agent-native proof — not "I used AI" but "I shipped products where agents are the primary user"

The role category literally exists at Stripe and Figma now. dr-cv's four cases (EnRegla, TE Skin, TE Black, Life Update Mobile) must each be tagged with which agent consumed/produced what. TE Skin ("agent-consumable DS") is Danilo's strongest asset here — it should lead the case grid, not sit third. The "profile + agentic AI = combination" framing from Danilo's own brand is exactly Linear's "teams and agents operate together." Mirror the phrase.
04
Tied to: Vercel · Cursor · Stripe (Design Engineer, Presence) · Work & Co

Shipped-in-code evidence — not just Figma mockups

Design Engineer is now a default role at Vercel, Cursor, Stripe, Linear. Every one of them filters for "prototype in code." dr-cv should have one interactive, live-code artifact embedded in the landing itself (not a link out), demonstrating shipped product thinking. The existing dr-cv repo (HTML/CSS/JS generators) is already this proof — surface it. "View source" on the landing should be a legitimate recruiting channel.
05
Tied to: Anthropic ("no bystanders") · Mistral ("leave your ego aside") · Stripe ("egoless collaboration") · Linear ("problem solvers and storytellers")

Profile-plus-agents framing — never solo-genius, always leverage + rigor

Every top company on this list filters against the lone-genius narrative. Anthropic: "none of us are bystanders." Mistral: "leave your ego aside." Stripe: "egoless collaboration." Danilo's own positioning ("profile + agentic AI = combination, avoid solo-genius") is pre-aligned with the market — the landing must make that explicit. The testimonials from Jennifer Sheppard and Alexander Giraldez must praise method + leverage, not raw talent. The honest mastered-vs-learning split is not a weakness; it is the same "evidence over credentials" rigor Anthropic names.
