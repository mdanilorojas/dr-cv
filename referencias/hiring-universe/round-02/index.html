dr-cv / hiring universe / round 02

Portfolios of people already inside the rooms Danilo wants in.

Fourteen portfolios from designers and design engineers currently at Vercel, Linear, Cursor, Raycast, Shopify, Klaviyo, Google Chrome DX, Radix/shadcn-adjacent, and independents who moonlight as tool-shapers. The goal is not to copy their aesthetic. It is to reverse-engineer the signal structure that gets a person hired into these rooms and translate it into a dr-cv that reads as peer, not petitioner.

14 portfolios surveyed 3 archetypes identified 5 concrete lessons target role . AI Product Designer / Agentic UX Lead

01The three portfolio archetypes among hires

Across all fourteen, three clean patterns emerge. Nobody who got hired at these companies is running a full-bleed agency-style case-grid. The template is always one of these three.

Archetype A

The Essayist-Builder

Extremely restrained layout. Almost no case studies. The signal is a body of writing plus shipped OSS. Hero is a single sentence of taste.

  • Long blog archive, sometimes hundreds of posts
  • GitHub-first, npm download counts as proof
  • Contact = email + X, no form, no CTA button
Members: Paco Coursey, Pedro Duarte, Rauno Freiberg, Shu Ding, Rasmus Andersson, John Otander, Max Stoiber, Lee Robinson
Archetype B

The Live-Demo Showcase

The portfolio is the work. Motion tier high. Every scroll unlocks an interactive piece the visitor can play with. Case studies are optional; the demo is the case study.

  • Embedded components, not screenshots
  • CodePen / Sonner / Vaul / demos as primary inventory
  • Availability signaled by cadence, not copy
Members: Emil Kowalski, Adam Argyle (nerdy.dev), Jhey Tompkins, Josh Comeau, Tim Holman
Archetype C

The Curated Case Grid

The most recognizable "portfolio" shape, but rare among hires. Named projects with numeric proof, a visible resume, and an availability target. Heavily disciplined to avoid agency bloat.

  • 3-9 projects, not 20
  • Concrete metrics ("100k+ installs", "6k stars")
  • "View Full Resume" link is present and obvious
Members: Brittany Chiang, Samuel Kraft

03Five concrete lessons for dr-cv

1

Kill the case-study grid. Ship a running manifesto + a named-OSS list.

Nine of fourteen portfolios (the whole Essayist cluster) have zero case studies and still got their authors hired at Vercel, Linear, Raycast, Cursor, Shopify. The mechanism that replaces case studies is always the same: a voice-forward hero sentence plus a short list of named artifacts the reader already recognizes.

Evidence: rauno.me — six-line manifesto, zero case studies. paco.me — one sentence + "cmdk, Next Themes, Writer" and nothing else. johno.com — one sentence + "MDX, Blocks, Tachyons, CSS Stats". For dr-cv: replace "Selected case studies" with "Selected shipped systems: EnRegla, Trusted Environments Skin, TE Black DS, Aura".
2

A live agent demo belongs above the fold, not behind a CTA.

The entire Live-Demo archetype treats the hero as an interactive artifact: Emil's Sonner runs in the page, Argyle's view-transition CSS fires on scroll, Jhey's CodePens embed directly. None of them write "Request a demo." They are the demo. For an Agentic UX target role, a static hero would be a costume failure.

Evidence: emilkowal.ski hero includes live Sonner + Vaul previews. nerdy.dev — "scroll-driven animations, view transitions, and interactive demos throughout site". joshwcomeau.com — animated keyframes on the hero illustration. For dr-cv: the first visible block must be a runnable agent conversation, not a photo.
3

Use counters and named metrics the way senior hires do — sparingly and specific.

Only three portfolios show numeric proof, and in every case it is one load-bearing number attached to a named artifact, not a dashboard of KPIs. Brittany's "100k+ installs" on Halcyon. Pedro's "Radix: 20M+ monthly downloads". Argyle's "346 notes, 88 blog posts, 41 talks, 138 podcast appearances" as running counters. No percentages, no "increased engagement by 37%" consulting-speak.

Evidence: brittanychiang.com — "Halcyon Theme reports 100k+ Installs; portfolio v4 notes 6k+ stars and 3k+ forks". ped.ro — "Radix (20M+ monthly downloads) sits in the bio sentence". For dr-cv: one counter per artifact. "EnRegla: 4 paying pilots." "TE Black DS: N components, a11y-audited." Resist the dashboard.
4

State availability explicitly. Open, closed, or selective — pick one and put it in the chrome.

Most portfolios leave availability implicit and that is a weakness we can exploit. Only two state it clearly — and both stand out because of it. Lee Robinson's "Reach out if interested, lee@leerob.com" is warm and explicit. John Otander's "not currently looking for new opportunities" is the inverse and reads as status. Either state is magnetic. The middle — a Contact form with nothing above it — is the worst.

Evidence: leerob.com — "Reach out if interested, lee@leerob.com". johno.com — "not currently looking for new opportunities". For dr-cv: a persistent status pill in the header. "Open to AI Product Designer / Agentic UX Lead roles. Reply to hello@..."
5

The target role goes on the site, not in the subject line of a cold email.

Brittany Chiang's site says "accessibility" before you scroll. Samuel Kraft's says "Design Engineer". Pedro Duarte's says "product-focused". The hires advertise the category of next role right where the employer's recruiter lands. None of them say "UX/UI Designer" — they say the specific specialty they want next. This is the single biggest delta between portfolios that get offers and portfolios that get "looks nice" replies.

Evidence: brittanychiang.com — "Role target visible: Yes — accessibility and component library leadership emphasized". samuelkraft.com — "Design Engineer positioning clear". For dr-cv: the hero must literally say "AI Product Designer / Agentic UX Lead" in a place the eye cannot miss. Not in the About page. In the header rule.
