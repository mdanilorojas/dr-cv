01Executive summary ≤ 300 words

Ten rounds of research asked one question: what should dr-cv actually look like, and how should it be built? Rounds 1–4 mapped the universe — thirty sites across AI landings, designer portfolios, studios, and awwwards winners — and found a recurring grammar: dark canvas, restrained accent, editorial typography, live data, inline case chapters. Round 5 surfaced four fresh techniques missing from the first sweep (cursor-scrubbed timelines, command palettes, bracket-nav, inline interactive widgets).

Rounds 6–8 were the verdict rounds. Round 6 killed WebGL maximalism: Tier LOW is enough — CSS + canvas 2D outperforms three.js at 1/40th the cost. Round 7 resolved palette + type: Warm Huly (near-black + apricot #FF8964, JetBrains Mono display + Inter body). Round 8 resolved structure: Proposal A (Restrained Huly, 7 sections, long-scroll) was the only proposal that absorbed Danilo's full content — 4 cases, 2 active clients, a 15-year arc, method, 2 real testimonials, 4 numeric proofs — without compromising any of them.

Round 9 distilled everything into six internally-coherent directions, ranked D1/D5/D3 as the top three. Round 10 converged: ship Final-1 · Warm Huly Refined. It is the only direction that aligns with every prior verdict simultaneously: tier LOW motion, Warm Huly palette, Proposal A structure, top-ranked fit, lowest cost, and a direct evolution of the existing generators/templates/huly-landing/ codebase. Final-2 (AI Product-Tour) is held as a secondary CTA inside Final-1; Final-3 (Editorial Craft) is held as a v2 evolution for once F1 is in production with real traffic data.

06The pick one paragraph · opinionated

Ship Final-1 · Warm Huly Refined.

F1 is the only direction that answers every question the research raised with a simultaneous yes. R6 said Tier LOW; F1 uses CSS + canvas 2D. R7 said Warm Huly; F1 is Warm Huly by name. R8 said Proposal A; F1 is Proposal A verbatim. R9 ranked it highest on fit × lowest on cost. When four independent rounds converge on the same answer, the correct move is to ship what the research already proved, not override it with taste. Distinctiveness is a second-order problem; existence is first-order. Hold Final-2's "artifact download" CTA as a secondary action inside F1's hero, and keep Final-3 as a v2 evolution for August once F1 has real traffic data to justify the editorial upgrade.
